Coupon Collecting

published April 01, 2026

The original coupon collector’s problem poses the question:

If each cereal box contains a random coupon uniformly drawn from nnn varieties, what is the probability that ttt boxes must be bought in order to collect each coupon?

A subtly different question arises when considering the case that one does not know the total number of coupons. How many boxes must be bought before you know you have all of them?

Of course, you can never be absolutely certain. But say you buy one billion boxes, and get 333,495,391333{,}495{,}391333,495,391 blue, 333,284,381333{,}284{,}381333,284,381 red, and 333,220,228333{,}220{,}228333,220,228 green coupons. I would be fairly confident that in fact there are three varieties of coupon! Here, you can test that intuition.
